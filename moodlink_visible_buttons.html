Understanding mud

So... what exactly is mud?

Mud is what happens when water mixes with earth. That's the simple science. But on MoodLink, we believe mud means much more than that — it shows up in your everyday life in ways you might never have noticed.

Think about it: when it rains and the ground gets soft and sticky underfoot, that's mud. When a potter shapes a bowl from clay, that's mud. When you put on a face mask made from volcanic earth, that's mud. And when you feel so sad or confused that you can't move forward — people often say they feel "stuck in the mud." That's mud too.

Mud is one of the most human things on Earth. For thousands of years, people have built homes with it, created art with it, healed their skin with it, played in it, and used it to describe their deepest feelings. MoodLink is here to explore all of it.

Physical mud
Actual soil + water found in nature, farms and rivers.
Emotional mud
Feelings that make you heavy, slow or stuck inside.
Beauty mud
Clay masks and natural skincare used across cultures.
Craft mud
Pottery clay shaped into bowls, art and bricks.
